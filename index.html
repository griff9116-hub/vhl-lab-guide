Sample Rejection Reasons

Understanding why samples are rejected helps prevent delays and repeat collections. Click each item to expand.

The specimen container carries no patient identifiers. Without a label, the sample cannot be safely linked to the request and will be rejected.

ResolutionRe-collect and label the tube at the point of collection with full patient name, date of birth and unique ID.

The name, date of birth or unique identifier on the tube does not match the details on the request form. This is a patient safety critical error.

ResolutionVerify patient identity and re-collect if discrepancy cannot be resolved with certainty.

The specimen was collected into an incorrect container (e.g. EDTA Purple received instead of Gold SST). Additives from one tube can interfere with analysis in another.

ResolutionRe-collect using the correct tube type as specified in the VHL tube guide above.

The sample was stored at the wrong temperature (e.g. frozen when it should be refrigerated, or left at ambient when it should be frozen). Temperature excursions may denature analytes.

ResolutionRe-collect following the storage requirements specified for the requested test in the catalogue.

The tube contains insufficient volume to perform the requested analysis. Under-filled EDTA and citrate tubes in particular will produce invalid results due to incorrect additive ratios.

ResolutionRe-collect ensuring the tube is filled to the indicated fill line or minimum volume.

Red blood cells have lysed, releasing intracellular contents (potassium, LDH, AST) into the serum/plasma and causing interference with multiple assays.

ResolutionRe-collect using correct venepuncture technique. Avoid prolonged tourniquet application and excessive tube agitation.

High lipid content (milky appearance) causes optical interference with photometric assays and may falsely elevate or suppress certain results.

ResolutionRe-collect from a fasting patient. If non-fasting is clinically required, contact the lab to discuss ultracentrifugation.

Elevated bilirubin causes yellow/green discolouration that can interfere with colorimetric assays. Results may be artificially suppressed or elevated.

ResolutionClinical — cannot be resolved by re-collection. Contact the lab for affected assays and discuss appropriate methodology.

The collection tube has passed its expiry date. Vacuum seal integrity and additive stability cannot be guaranteed beyond the stated date.

ResolutionCheck tube expiry before use. Discard expired containers and replace stock.

The test request form is absent or lacks essential details (patient name, DOB, tests requested, clinical information). Testing cannot proceed without a complete request.

ResolutionResubmit sample with a fully completed request form. Portal requests automatically capture all required fields.

Excessive time elapsed between collection and receipt at the laboratory. Delayed samples may yield spurious results for labile analytes such as glucose, potassium and coagulation factors.

ResolutionUse the VHL courier service or dispatch samples immediately after collection. Contact the lab if urgent testing is required.

Submission Process

Follow these steps to ensure your sample is received and processed without delay.

1

Complete a test request

Submit via the VHL online portal or use a paper request form. All required patient and test fields must be completed.

2

Label specimens at point of collection

Label every tube immediately after collection with the patient's full name, date of birth and unique ID. Never pre-label tubes.

3

Package for transport

Place specimens in a sealed primary container with absorbent material, then into the biohazard transport bag. Use triple packaging for Category B specimens.

4

Dispatch via courier or bring to clinic

Hand to the VHL courier, drop off at a clinic reception, or book a mobile phlebotomist who will transport the sample for you.

5

Track and receive results

Log in to the VHL portal 24/7 to track your specimen status. You'll receive a notification when results are available, complete with reference ranges.
